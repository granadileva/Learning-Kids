NOUN

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are the fundamental building blocks of sentences, acting as the subject, object, or other parts of a sentence that represent a person, place, thing, or idea.

Examples:

Person – Names of people (e.g., John, teacher, doctor).
Place – Names of places (e.g., school, park, India).
Thing – Names of objects (e.g., table, dog, apple).
Idea – Names of feelings or concepts (e.g., happiness, love, bravery).

Types of Nouns:
Common Nouns: General names for people, places, or things (e.g., city, book, dog)
Proper Nouns: Specific names for people, places, or things (e.g., Manila, The Philippines, John)
Concrete Nouns: Things that can be perceived by the senses (e.g., table, chair, book)
Abstract Nouns: Things that cannot be perceived by the senses (e.g., love, happiness, freedom)
Collective Nouns: Words that refer to a group of people or things (e.g., team, band, herd)

Function in a Sentence:
Nouns can function as:
Subject: The person or thing doing the action (e.g., The dog barks.)
Object: The person or thing receiving the action (e.g., I saw the cat.)
Complement: Provides information about the subject or object (e.g., She is a doctor.)
Modifier: Describes another noun (e.g., A red car.)

Examples of Noun in Sentences

PERSON

1. The doctor prescribed medicine for the patient.

  • Doctor: Common noun (a generalc name for a person).

  • Medicine: Common noun (a general name for a substance).

  • Patient: Common noun (a general name for a person).

PLACE

2. Mount Everest is the tallest mountain in the world.

  • Mount Everest: Proper noun (a specific name of a place).

  • Mountain: Common noun (a general name for a landform).

THINGS

3. The books are separate.

  • Books: Common noun (a general name for objects).

IDEA

4. Happiness is often found in small moments.

  • Happiness: Abstract noun (a concept or idea).

  • Moments: Common noun (a general name for units and time).
