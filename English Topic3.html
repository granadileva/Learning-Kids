VERBS

A verb is a word that shows an action, a state of being, or an occurrence. It tells us what someone or something is doing, feeling, or experiencing. Verbs are very important because they help us create sentences and express ideas.

Types of Verbs:

  1. Action Verbs: These verbs show what someone or something is doing.
  2. Linking Verbs: These verbs connect the subject to a word that describes or identifies it. They don’t show action.
  3. Helping Verbs (Auxiliary Verbs): These verbs help the main verb to show tense or possibility.
  4. Modal Verbs: These verbs show possibility, ability, permission, or obligation.

Examples


Action Verbs (verbs that show action):
Examples:
  • Run
  • Jump
  • Eat
  • Play
  • Sing
  • Dance
Sentences:
  • The dog runs in the park.
  • She sings a song.
  • They play soccer.

Linking Verbs (verbs that describe a state of being):
Examples:
  • Is
  • Am
  • Are
  • Was
  • Were
  • Seem
  • Feel
Sentences:
  • He is happy.
  • The flowers are beautiful.
  • She feels tired.
Helping Verbs (verbs that help the main verb):
Examples:
  • Have
  • Has
  • Had
  • Will
  • Shall
  • Can
  • Could
Sentences:
  • I will go to the park.
  • She has eaten her lunch.
  • They can swim very well.
Modal Verbs (verbs that show possibility or necessity):
Examples:
  • Can
  • Could
  • May
  • Might
  • Must
  • Should
Sentences:
  • You must finish your homework.
  • She can ride a bike.
  • They should clean their room.
