ARITHMETIC OPERATIONS

Arithmetic operations are the basic mathematical processes used to calculate and manipulate numbers. These operations form the foundation of mathematics and are essential for solving everyday problems.

The four primary arithmetic operations are:

  1. Addition (+): Combining two or more numbers to get a total.
  2. Subtraction (−): Taking one number away from another.
  3. Multiplication (×): Repeated addition of a number a certain number of times.
  4. Division (÷): Splitting a number into equal parts or groups.

Examples


Addition:

Example: ( 4 + 3 = 7 )


Explanation: If you have 4 apples and you add 3 more, you have a total of 7 apples.


Subtraction:

Examples: ( 10 - 6 = 4 )


Explanation: If you have 10 candies and you eat 6, you have 4 candies left.


Multiplication:

Example: ( 5 x 3 = 15 )


Explanation: If you have 5 bags with 3 oranges in each bag, you have a total of 15 oranges.


Division:

Example: ( 12 ÷ 4 = 3 )


Explanation: If you have 12 cookies and you want to share them equally among 4 friends, each friend gets 3 cookies.
